The Science behind Kinedu

We've partnered with experts from Harvard, Stanford, and MIT to make sure everything in Kinedu is grounded in the latest research on early childhood development.

01

Baby development in a nutshell

Early experiences form the foundation for future health and wellbeing — they literally build the architecture of the developing brain.

When children interact with engaged and responsive adults, and are allowed to play and experiment, they thrive. These interactions — called serve and return — shape brain circuitry and form the basis from which children learn to interact with the world.

Learn more from Harvard's Center on the Developing Child →
02

Developmental playtime activities

Every activity in Kinedu is developed by our team of early learning experts with two goals: generate playtime ideas that develop specific milestones, and create serve-and-return interactions that strengthen your bond with your baby.

In 2016, we partnered with Prof. Michael Frank from Stanford University and found that our activities lead to real increases in parents' verbal and non-verbal engagement with their children.

Stanford University · Prof. Michael Frank
03

Kinedu Skills® Milestone Model

With support from MIT-Solve and our research partner Prof. Michael Frank, we leveraged millions of milestone observations to develop our innovative Early Childhood Milestone Model.

Kinedu Skills® is different from other assessments because it shows that development is multi-factorial, interdependent, and continuous. Cognitive, linguistic, social-emotional, and physical areas all interact and build upon each other.
