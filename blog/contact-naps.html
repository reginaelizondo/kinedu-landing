For new parents, there is nothing more precious than the sight of their newborn baby sleeping peacefully. However, getting a baby to sleep can be a real challenge, especially when they are going through the early stages of development. That is why many parents are turning to contact naps as a gentle and effective way to help their babies sleep. 

In this article, we will explore what contact naps are and how they can benefit your little one’s sleeping patterns.

What are Contact Naps?

Contact napping is a term used to describe the practice of holding your baby while they sleep. This can involve placing your baby in a sling, carrier, or simply holding them in your arms. The idea behind contact napping is to provide your baby with the close physical contact and warmth they need to feel safe and secure while they sleep. This can be especially beneficial for newborn babies who are used to being inside their mother’s body during pregnancy.

Contact napping is different from traditional sleep training methods, which focus on helping babies learn to self-soothe and fall asleep on their own. Instead, this is a more natural approach that takes into account the needs of the baby and the parent. By holding your baby while they sleep, you are providing them with the comfort and security they need to relax and sleep peacefully.

Why Can Contact Naps Be Good for Your Newborn Baby?

There are several reasons why contact naps can be beneficial for your baby’s development and well-being. Here are a few of the key benefits:

1. Contact Naps Promote Bonding and Attachment

One of the most significant benefits of contact napping is that it promotes bonding and secure attachment between the parent and the baby. When you hold your baby close, you are providing them with a sense of security and warmth that helps them feel safe and loved. This can have a positive impact on your baby’s emotional and psychological development and help build a strong parent-child bond.

2. They Help to Regulate Your Baby’s Body Temperature

Newborn babies are not yet able to regulate their body temperature effectively. As a result, they can easily become too hot or too cold, which can disrupt their sleep patterns. By holding your baby close during a contact nap, you are helping to regulate their body temperature and keep them comfortable. This can lead to longer and more restful sleep for your baby.

3. They Can Help to Soothe Your Baby’s Crying

Babies cry for a variety of reasons, including hunger, discomfort, and tiredness. When your baby is feeling upset or distressed, holding them close during can be a comforting and soothing experience. The close physical contact can help calm your baby and reduce their crying, which can improve their overall sleep quality.

4. They Can Help Your Baby Transition to Independent Sleep

While contact napping is not a traditional sleep training method, it can help your baby transition to independent sleep over time. As your baby gets older and more accustomed to sleeping on their own, you can gradually reduce the amount of contact napping you do. This can help your baby develop the skills they need to fall asleep independently while still feeling safe and secure.

How to Practice Contact Napping Safely

While contact napping can be a wonderful way to bond with your baby and help them sleep, it is important to practice it safely. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

1. Use a Carrier or Sling That is Appropriate for Your Baby’s Age and Weight

When choosing a carrier or sling, make sure it is appropriate for your baby’s age and weight. Follow the manufacturer’s instructions carefully and ensure that your baby is securely fastened in the carrier.

2. Keep Your Baby’s Airway Clear

It is essential to keep your baby’s airway clear when they are sleeping in a carrier or sling. Make sure that their face is visible and not covered by fabric, and ensure that their chin is off their chest to avoid any obstruction of their airway.

3. Check Your Baby’s Position Regularly

When your baby is sleeping in a carrier or sling, it can be challenging to monitor their position. Make sure to check on your baby frequently and adjust their position if necessary to ensure that their airway is clear and that they are comfortable.

4. Avoid Overheating

Contact napping can be an effective way to regulate your baby’s body temperature, but it is essential to avoid overheating. Make sure that your baby is not dressed too warmly and that the carrier or sling allows for good airflow.

5. Stay Alert and Awake

It is important to stay alert and awake when contact napping with your baby. While it may be tempting to close your eyes and drift off to sleep, it is essential to remain vigilant and attentive to your baby’s needs.

If you feel sleepy, take a break and find a safe and comfortable place to rest. It is not safe to fall asleep with your baby in a carrier or sling as it can increase the risk of suffocation and other hazards.

When to Stop Contact Naps?

Knowing when to stop contact naps is just as important as knowing how to start them safely. While contact naps can be an effective way to help your baby sleep and promote bonding, there comes a time when they are no longer necessary.

As your baby grows and develops, they may become more independent and prefer to sleep on their own. It is also important to transition your baby to a safe sleep environment, such as a crib or bassinet, when they reach a certain age or weight. Experts recommend that babies should sleep in their own sleep environment by the age of six months, or when they weigh around 18 pounds.

If you find it challenging to transition your baby to sleeping on their own, start by gradually reducing the amount of contact napping and increasing the time they spend in their crib or bassinet. Remember, it may take time for your baby to adjust, so be patient and consistent.

In conclusion, contact naps can be an effective way to help your baby sleep and promote bonding, but they should not be used indefinitely. It is essential to transition your baby to a safe sleep environment when they reach a certain age or weight and establish healthy sleep habits. By doing so, you can ensure that your baby gets the restful sleep they need to grow and develop while promoting a strong and loving bond between you and your little one.
